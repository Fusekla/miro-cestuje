Base

Santillana del Mar
Mayorazgo de Altamira Mila
Beautiful village, but heavily touristic. Plan your dinners before you go — don't wing it every night.

Dinner within driving distance (15–25 min):
· El Nuevo Molino, Puente Arce (20 min) — 18th-century water mill on the River Pas, serious Cantabrian cooking, garden with chapel. Former Michelin star. Reserve in advance. Good for one proper evening out.
· La Dársena de Suances, Suances (15 min) — fresh fish and seafood right next to the beach, good value, local atmosphere. A reliable fallback for any evening.
· El Pasaje de los Nobles, Santillana itself — 4th-generation family restaurant, genuinely well-regarded, gluten-free focused. Consistently rated the best in Santillana. Worth knowing for nights you don't want to drive.

No fish smell (the guide will explain that good anchovies shouldn't taste fishy either). The curing process takes months — it's craftsmanship, not a conveyor belt. The town is completely un-touristed and feels like real Cantabria.

Beaches · nearby options

For afternoons or unplanned days

Playa de Santa Justa (Ubiarco, 10 min) — Small secluded cove with a dramatic rock arch, often uncrowded even in summer. Closest quiet beach to base. Good for an afternoon impulse.

Playa de Arnía (25 min east) — Karst limestone rock formations, shallow turquoise pool at high tide, spectacular coastal scenery. Increasingly visited but still calm in September. The most visually striking beach on this coastline.

Playa de Langre (30 min east) — Wide crescent of sand between green cliffs, no tourist facilities, natural and wild. Good for a walk rather than a structured beach day. Popular with anglers.

Comillas beach (20 min west) — Sheltered bay, calmer water, Blue Flag. Good for a relaxed swim if you're already in Comillas. Convenient rather than spectacular.

Oyambre (already in the itinerary, 25 min west) — Also works as a standalone afternoon beach any other day. Fine sand, dune backdrop, open sea.

Optional swap · Asturias

Llanes

Drive ~1h. Old town with a well-preserved medieval wall, working fishing harbor, and the remarkable Cubos de la Memoria — concrete breakwater cubes painted by local artists, genuinely striking at low tide. Skip the cider-house angle. Use only if you feel rested and want a longer day. Not mandatory.

Optional add · East Coast

Castro Urdiales

~1.5h east of Santillana. Gothic church dramatically perched on a sea cliff, working fishing harbor, almost no package tourists. Very different atmosphere from anywhere else on the itinerary — quieter, more dramatic coastline. Could replace Santander if you want a lower-key city day, or slot in as its own half-day if energy allows.
