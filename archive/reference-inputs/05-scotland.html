Planning notes & flags
⚠ Book now — Jacobite Steam Train

Both directions (Fort William → Mallaig and return) sell out months ahead. Book as soon as possible if not already done. Only a few trains run daily on this route.

✓ Day 6 logistics — fully managed by operator (West Coast Motors)

All transport on Day 6 is handled end-to-end: ferry tickets, coach across Mull, and boat. Show the booking email at each stage. Arrive at Oban ferry terminal by 06:55 — stay within easy walking distance of the terminal.

⚠ Iona Abbey — advance booking required

If anyone wants to visit the abbey interior, book online in advance at historicenvironment.scot. You have approximately 2 hours on Iona; slots fill up.

⚠ West Highland Line — train times constrain Glasgow stop (Day 3)

Only a handful of trains run Glasgow → Oban daily. Check the timetable: this will determine how long you realistically have in Glasgow. Don't miss the right train.

⚠ Day 12–13 — tight turnaround into departure morning

Arriving Edinburgh by evening train on Day 12, with a 09:00 flight on Day 13, means being at the airport around 07:00. Consider staying near the airport or very central. Early alarm required.

Note — Glenfinnan appears twice (Days 8 & 9)

Day 8 passes the Glenfinnan Viaduct by train; Day 9 visits it on foot. This is intentional and works well — two very different experiences — but worth flagging so it doesn't feel like a scheduling error when reviewing the plan.

Note — No true rest day in the schedule

Day 6 (Staffa/Lunga/Iona) is the most physically and logistically demanding day of the trip, and it comes mid-sequence with full days on either side. The "easy" days (Oban Day 4, Cairngorms morning) provide some relief but may not be enough for all five people. Worth discussing with the group.
